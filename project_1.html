E-Commerce UX Case Study

Reducing Checkout Friction in Indian E-Commerce

Snapdeal Checkout Redesign

Opportunity Type: Linear

This project represents a Linear Opportunity—focusing on the continuous, iterative improvement of an existing core product. By streamlining Snapdeal's checkout flow, we are removing friction from an established user journey to maximize conversion efficiency.

Executive Summary

Our goal was to overhaul Snapdeal’s mobile checkout to cut cart abandonment and boost conversions. We analyzed user behavior and the Indian e-commerce context, audited the existing Snapdeal flow, and benchmarked top competitors for best practices.

Key findings included rampant checkout drop-offs (globally ~70% abandonment) and strong user demand for trust cues and simplicity. We defined clear personas and mapped their journeys. Through heuristic review, we identified major pain points—hidden costs, form overload, weak CTA hierarchy, and confusion around payment options.

We envisioned a streamlined, mobile-first checkout: a concise 3–4 step flow with autofill support and minimal fields. We introduced trust signals and microcopy to reassure users. The new design promises simpler steps, transparent pricing, and greater trust – all critical to turning browsers into buyers in India’s value-driven market.

Problem Statement

Snapdeal faced high cart abandonment in its mobile checkout. In India, these issues are amplified by the “Bharat” shopper: mobile-first, price-sensitive users who demand trust (e.g. COD or UPI options). We needed to simplify the flow, make costs clear, and build trust, so more users complete payment.

Background Context

Indian E-Commerce Landscape: India’s online market is massive, with over 85% of Snapdeal orders coming from mobile devices. However, trust issues persist: Cash-on-Delivery (CoD) remains extremely common (60–65% of orders). UPI payments have soared, but CoD is a vital trust option in smaller towns.

Snapdeal’s Market: Snapdeal caters to the value-conscious middle class. Over 70% of customers are 18–45 year-olds earning ₹3–10L/year. These users prioritize free shipping and CoD to reduce payment friction. They expect a fast, easy checkout on slow connections.

UX Audit & Pain Points

We performed an audit of Snapdeal’s existing checkout to uncover pain points:

  • Lengthy Flow: >4 screens (cart, login, address, payment, review).
  • Hidden Costs: Extra fees appeared late in the flow, leading to abandonment.
  • Forced Login: Mandatory sign-in deters ~26% of shoppers.
  • Form Friction: Weak autofill/hint support on forms.
  • Payment Confusion: CoD option lacked clarity, UPI options were buried.
  • Lack of Trust Cues: Few reassuring messages or security markers.

Design Strategy & Solutions

Mobile-First Layout

We adopted an 8px baseline grid with touch targets ≥44x44dp. Primary CTAs ("Place Order", "Pay ₹X") are full-width, high-contrast orange buttons fixed at the bottom of the screen.

Trust & Security Design

On the payment screen, a lock icon next to the "Pay Now" button signals SSL encryption. Return policy links and estimated delivery dates are shown early in the process. We labeled CoD clearly: "Cash on Delivery (Home payment) – ₹30 fee" with an explanatory tooltip.

UX Writing & Error Recovery

We crafted action-oriented copy. Instead of vague errors, inline validation guides users instantly (e.g. "We deliver only to certain pincodes. Please re-enter."). Empty states encourage browsing ("Your cart is empty! Find great deals...").

Metrics & KPIs

North Star Metric: Checkout Conversion Rate
The primary indicator of success for this redesign is the percentage of initiated checkouts that successfully convert into completed orders. Reducing the current ~70% abandonment rate is our main objective.

Success Metrics

  • Cart Abandonment Rate: Tracking the overall reduction of drop-offs during the checkout flow.
  • Payment Success Rate: Percentage of started payment attempts that succeed without errors.
  • Average Checkout Time: Time taken from clicking "Checkout" to the final "Order Placed" screen.
  • Drop-off by Step: Funnel analysis to pinpoint exactly where users abandon (e.g., Address vs. Payment).
  • User Satisfaction (NPS): Post-checkout surveys to measure perceived confidence and trust.

A/B Testing Ideas

To continually iterate and optimize, we propose testing:

  • Single-Page vs Multi-Page Checkout flows.
  • Trust Badge Emphasis (e.g., testing the impact of prominent SSL lock icons).
  • Listing UPI vs CoD first in payment options to observe shifting preferences.
