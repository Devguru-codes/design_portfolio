Automating Meeting Documentation with AI

Meeting Minutes AI - From Live Call to Structured Notes in One Click

Opportunity Type: Transformational

This represents a Transformational Opportunity. It takes an entirely manual, time-consuming process (attending meetings, taking notes, distributing minutes) and replaces it with a fully automated AI pipeline: a bot joins your Google Meet, records audio, transcribes speech with speaker identification, and generates structured meeting minutes with action items.

Executive Summary

Professionals spend an average of 23 hours per week in meetings, yet 73% of employees admit to doing other work during meetings (Harvard Business Review). The result: incomplete notes, forgotten action items, and no institutional memory. Meeting minutes, when they exist, are written by a designated note-taker who misses parts of the discussion while writing.

Meeting Minutes AI solves this by automating the entire lifecycle. A Selenium-driven bot silently joins a Google Meet session. System audio is captured via WASAPI loopback (no virtual audio cables needed). The recording is then processed through a two-stage pipeline: Faster Whisper for word-level transcription, and pyannote.audio for speaker diarization (identifying who said what). Finally, an LLM (Groq's Llama-3 70B) generates structured minutes with sections for Summary, Key Discussion Points, Decisions Made, Action Items, and Notable Quotes.

A built-in Q&A interface lets users ask questions about the meeting ("What did the marketing team decide about the Q3 budget?") and receive cited answers grounded in the transcript.

Problem Statement

Teams lose critical information from meetings because manual note-taking is incomplete and biased. A designated note-taker captures only 40-60% of the discussion. Action items are frequently lost, with 63% of meetings ending without clear next steps. There is no searchable archive of past discussions, forcing teams to rely on collective memory.

Background Context

The Meeting Productivity Crisis: The average professional attends 62 meetings per month. 71% of those meetings are considered unproductive (Atlassian). The root cause isn't the meetings themselves but the lack of proper documentation and follow-through. Tools like Otter.ai and Fireflies.ai exist but cost $16-30/user/month, adding up quickly for teams.

Technical Landscape: Open-source STT models (Whisper) have reached production quality. Speaker diarization (pyannote) can identify distinct speakers with 90%+ accuracy. LLMs can now summarize long transcripts reliably. The missing piece was an integrated pipeline that connects these components end-to-end with a simple user interface.

UX Audit & Pain Points

We interviewed 15 professionals across startups and mid-size companies to understand their meeting documentation workflow:

  • Manual Note-Taking: One person is "sacrificed" to take notes, missing parts of the discussion. Notes are subjective and incomplete.
  • No Speaker Attribution: Existing recording tools produce a wall of text. You can't tell who said what without listening to the entire recording.
  • Action Item Amnesia: Action items discussed verbally are forgotten within 24 hours if not immediately captured in a task manager.
  • No Searchable Archive: Past meeting discussions are buried in Google Docs or Slack threads. Finding "what we decided about X in the March meeting" is nearly impossible.
  • Cost Barrier: Enterprise meeting AI tools (Otter, Fireflies, Grain) are expensive at scale. A 50-person company pays $800-1500/month.

Design Process & User Research

User Segmentation

We identified three primary user personas:

  • The Project Manager: Needs structured minutes with clear action items and owner assignments. Wants to distribute minutes via email within 5 minutes of the meeting ending.
  • The Team Lead: Wants a searchable archive of past decisions. Often needs to reference "what we agreed on" weeks later during standup disagreements.
  • The Individual Contributor: Wants to focus on the discussion without worrying about note-taking. Needs a way to catch up on meetings they missed.

Design Strategy & Solutions

End-to-End Automation Pipeline

The core design principle is zero manual intervention. The user pastes a Google Meet link, clicks one button, and gets structured minutes. The pipeline handles everything: joining the meeting silently, recording system audio, transcribing with speaker labels, generating minutes via LLM, and offering a Q&A interface.

Five-Tab Gradio Interface

We designed a tab-based UI that mirrors the pipeline stages, giving users visibility into each step while keeping the workflow linear:

flowchart LR A["Join and Record"] --> B["Transcribe"] B --> C["Generate Minutes"] C --> D["Q and A Chat"] E["Upload and Process"] --> B

Speaker Diarization Strategy

Raw transcription produces a wall of text. We integrated pyannote.audio's speaker diarization pipeline to segment audio by speaker. The merge algorithm maps Whisper's word-level timestamps to pyannote's speaker segments, producing a transcript formatted as "SPEAKER_01 [00:02:15]: We should prioritize the mobile app redesign."

LLM Summarization Prompt Design

We crafted a structured prompt template that instructs the LLM to output minutes in a consistent Markdown format with six mandatory sections: Meeting Overview, Summary, Key Discussion Points, Decisions Made, Action Items (with owners), and Notable Quotes. This ensures every meeting produces the same structured output.

Key Technical Challenges & Solutions

  • WASAPI Loopback: Recording system audio on Windows without virtual cables. We used PyAudioWPatch to hook directly into the WASAPI loopback device, capturing all desktop audio including Google Meet output.
  • Chrome Profile Auth: Google Meet blocks unrecognized bots. We use undetected-chromedriver with a real Chrome profile to bypass bot detection and join meetings seamlessly.
  • Groq Rate Limiting: The Groq API has aggressive rate limits. We implemented automatic API key rotation across multiple keys to handle long transcripts without hitting limits.
  • Speaker Overlap: When two people talk simultaneously, diarization accuracy drops. We tuned pyannote's segmentation threshold to handle cross-talk in typical meeting scenarios.

Metrics & KPIs

North Star Metric: Time from Meeting End to Minutes Distributed
The primary indicator of success is how quickly structured minutes are available after a meeting ends. Our target is under 3 minutes for a 1-hour meeting, compared to the current 30-60 minute manual baseline.

Success Metrics

  • Transcription Accuracy (WER): Word Error Rate of the Whisper transcription compared to manual transcripts (target: under 10%).
  • Speaker Identification Accuracy: Percentage of correctly attributed speaker segments (target: 85%+).
  • Action Item Recall: Percentage of verbally discussed action items successfully captured in the generated minutes.
  • Q&A Relevance: User satisfaction with answers provided by the meeting Q&A interface.
  • Adoption Rate: Number of meetings processed per week per user, indicating workflow integration.
